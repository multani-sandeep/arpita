## Interviewer Profile — Ajay Singh & Nilesh Patel

Derived from the questions asked in round 2.

- **Client relationship depth** — asked both "how do you work with different clients" and "how do you build relationships" separately. Double coverage of the same theme signals this was a live concern, likely carried forward from round 1.

- **Retention and commitment** — out-of-work gap, CDM vs SCDM, Worldpay history all touched this theme from different angles. The concern is whether she stays once trained and embedded.

**Style**

- Direct and probing — willing to follow up within the same question (colleague conflict probe, CDM downgrade test).
- Nilesh Patel's presence as an active SCDM is likely a calibration exercise: would she be credible at peer level with someone already in the role?
- Questions lean situational and reflective rather than purely technical — they are assessing judgment and maturity, not just experience.

## Signal Analysis — CDM Offer + Gap Question in the Same Interview

The user flagged a concern: being asked about development gaps (Q19) and offered CDM instead of SCDM (Q17) in the same interview.

This combination is not coincidental. Read together, it forms a pattern:

1. **Gap question first** — "what would you need to work on" is a temperature check on self-awareness AND a way to surface whether the candidate sees herself as ready for the level. Naming "delivery" and "escalation" as unqualified gaps, even unintentionally, may have signalled to Ajay that she sees core SCDM competencies as development areas rather than proven strengths.

2. **CDM offer second** — following a gap answer that touched delivery and escalation with a downgrade offer is a strong signal that seniority fit was a live concern in the room. It may have been: (a) a genuine alternative they have headcount for; (b) a resilience test to see if she holds the SCDM line; or (c) a signal that the interview had not fully resolved their seniority concerns.

3. **The answer she gave** — "haven't thought about it, needs to make financial sense" did not hold the line. Combined with unqualified delivery and escalation gaps earlier, the overall signal to the panel may have been that she is unsure of her own level.

**What this means for next steps**

If there is a round 3 or a further conversation, the priority is to reframe seniority clearly and early. She should not wait to be asked. The deliberate return to Worldpay, the Shell GO+ scale, the accountability to Technology and Marketing Directors, and the 15-year pattern of client-facing delivery at programme level are the evidence. The SCDM level is where she has been operating — not where she is aspiring to reach.

### 8. You are going live. NFRs weren't shared. Transactions are taking over a minute. What do you do?

Shell data ingestion (batch pipeline) is also a different scenario from live transaction latency (active production incident with immediate merchant impact). Stronger structure: stabilise first (live impact, can anything be mitigated or rerouted), communicate to client with honest timeline, identify constraints, fix and validate to NFR, close the process gap post-incident. Shell story can anchor steps 3–4 but only after showing you understand the live incident response.

### 20. Which do you think is better — Waterfall or Agile?

**Answer given:** Depends on the organisation. Finance and regulatory environments need clear scope defined and signed off upfront — Waterfall is better. Where the business needs to change direction, Agile gives visibility for the next few sprints and flexibility to descope and bring in new items.

**Assessment:** Right principle — not being dogmatic about methodology is a mature signal. The framing (org type drives the choice) is credible. However the answer stayed at a textbook level with no real example and no Worldpay-specific angle.

The stronger version at SCDM level:

- **Anchor to real experience** — Shell GO+ used both within the same programme: stage-gated programme governance with go/no-go at Steering per market (waterfall-adjacent), with sprint-based delivery within each market phase (Agile). That hybrid is the norm at enterprise scale, not a compromise.
- **Worldpay-specific context** — POS certification and scheme mandate deadlines are fixed and non-negotiable; that workstream is effectively Waterfall regardless of how the rest of the programme runs. Enterprise merchant implementations often blend: defined integration scope with Agile delivery cadence.
- **Senior framing** — the choice is rarely organisation-wide; it is workstream by workstream within the same programme. Regulatory or compliance workstreams, vendor certification cycles, and fixed go-live commitments all warrant Waterfall discipline. Discovery, product iteration, and evolving requirements suit Agile. A senior CDM holds both simultaneously.
