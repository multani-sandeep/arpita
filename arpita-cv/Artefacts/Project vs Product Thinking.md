# Project vs Product Thinking — giffgaff Interview Reference

> giffgaff must-have: "Strong understanding of the difference between empowered Product Teams (Discovery + Delivery) and traditional Feature Teams. Ability to coach teams away from project thinking."

---

## Compare and Contrast

| Dimension | Project Thinking | Product-Led / Outcome Delivery |
|---|---|---|
| Success measure | On time, on budget, on scope | Outcome achieved, customer value delivered |
| Team model | Feature team — assigned tasks to deliver | Empowered team — owns discovery AND delivery |
| Planning unit | Milestones, phases, Gantt chart | OKRs, sprint goals, continuous roadmap |
| Work definition | Requirements handed down | Problem space explored, solution shaped by team |
| PM/DM role | Drives execution, defines the plan | Enables delivery, removes blockers, protects team autonomy |
| Progress language | Deliverables complete, % done | Hypothesis tested, outcome moved, metric shifted |
| Prioritisation | Scope agreed upfront, change controlled | Backlog shaped by value and learning, reprioritised frequently |
| Stakeholder reporting | RAG status, milestone tracking | Outcome progress, risks to value, dependency health |
| Team autonomy | Low — prescribed scope and approach | High — team decides how to solve the problem |
| Coaching focus | Process compliance, RAID hygiene | Outcome ownership, experimentation, cross-team alignment |
| Discovery | Separate or absent | Continuous, embedded in team |
| Feedback loops | End of phase / at go-live | Iterative, within sprint or release cycle |

## Language Guide — Terms to Use and Avoid

| Use | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Outcome-led delivery | Deliverable completion |
| Coaching teams toward value | Driving teams to hit milestones |
| Protecting team autonomy | Assigning work to teams |
| Shared outcomes | Project scope |
| Enable and align | Control and track |
| Continuous prioritisation | Change control |

## Worked Example — Costa Coffee Data Platform

The data platform had 12+ ingestion sources to connect. The same work looks different through each lens:

**Project thinking**

- Backlog item: *"Build ingestion connector for source 7"*
- Sprint goal: *"Complete connectors for sources 7, 8, 9"*
- Prioritisation driver: technical sequence or original requirements list
- Success at end of sprint: connectors built and deployed
- Stakeholder update: *"6 of 12 connectors complete, on track for Q2"*

**Product-led thinking**

- Backlog item: *"Finance can't trust monthly cost reports — data is 3 months stale"*
- Sprint goal: *"Finance team can run their weekly report from live data"*
- Prioritisation driver: which source gap is causing the most business pain right now
- Success at end of sprint: finance team stops manually reconciling; reporting lag measurably reduced
- Stakeholder update: *"Reporting lag down from months to days; finance now self-serving"*

The difference is the **unit of work**. Project thinking ships connectors. Product thinking asks which connector unblocks a real decision first — the sequence of the 12 sources is driven by that, not by a pre-agreed build plan.
