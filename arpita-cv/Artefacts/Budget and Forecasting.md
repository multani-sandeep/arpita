### **Budget and Forecasting - PM / SPM View**

**Context**
- Engagement point: all 15 steps - SPM owns or is accountable across the full lifecycle
- Primary accountabilities: budget baseline (step 6), governance approval (step 7), rolling forecasts (step 11), portfolio reviews (step 14)
- Works across portfolio, programme and delivery levels
- Organisation model: product-led, portfolio-funded Agile - budget tied to products and platforms, not individual projects

| Step | Budgeting & Forecasting | SPM Role (what you specifically do) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **1\. Strategic planning** | Leadership defines priorities for products and platforms | Contribute delivery perspective, highlight feasibility, risks and delivery dependencies |
| **2\. Portfolio funding allocation** | Budget is allocated across portfolios based on strategic value | Provide input on delivery capacity, timelines and cost implications of proposed initiatives |
| **3\. Product and initiative prioritisation** | Epics and initiatives are prioritised on value and impact | Help translate priorities into delivery plans, highlight sequencing, dependencies and delivery trade-offs |
| **4\. Team and capacity planning** | Agile squads are assigned; budget is tied to cost of running these teams | Coordinate with engineering and delivery teams to confirm resourcing, identify gaps and ensure realistic capacity planning |
| **5\. High-level estimation** | Work is estimated using Agile methods such as story points or sizing techniques | Facilitate estimation sessions, challenge assumptions and ensure estimates are realistic and consistent across teams |
| **6\. Budget baseline creation** | Financial baseline is created based on team cost multiplied by time | Consolidate delivery estimates, team costs and timelines into a coherent budget and delivery baseline |
| **7\. Governance approval** | Budget and delivery plan are reviewed and approved by finance and leadership | Prepare governance packs, present budget, forecasts and risks and support approvals through clear reporting |
| **8\. Delivery begins (Agile execution)** | Teams deliver in sprints or continuous flow, consuming budget at a steady rate | Track delivery progress across streams, manage dependencies, unblock teams and ensure delivery stays aligned to plan |
| **9\. Spend tracking** | Actual spend is tracked against allocated budget | Monitor actuals vs budget, track burn rate and flag variances early to stakeholders |
| **10\. Progress tracking** | Progress is measured using Agile metrics such as velocity and burn-down | Track progress using burn-down, burn-up and milestone tracking and translate delivery status into clear reporting |
| **11\. Forecasting (rolling)** | Forecasts are continuously updated using velocity, backlog and burn rate | Produce rolling forecasts (EAC, ETC), predict delivery timelines and budget outcomes and update stakeholders regularly |
| **12\. Variance analysis** | Compare planned vs actual spend and delivery to identify gaps | Analyse deviations, explain variances and identify root causes in cost, scope or delivery performance |
| **13\. Adjustments** | Adjust scope, team size or timelines to stay within budget or maximise value | Recommend and implement adjustments such as scope reprioritisation, resourcing changes or timeline shifts |
| **14\. Portfolio and governance review** | Regular reviews assess value delivered and whether funding should continue | Present status, forecasts and risks to governance forums and support decision making on continuation or changes |
| **15\. Benefits realisation** | Outcomes such as improved platform performance, adoption or efficiency are measured | Track delivery outcomes and support reporting on whether intended benefits and business value have been achieved |

### **What exactly do you forecast?**

1. **Delivery Timelines**
   - Multi-stream delivery across: Product, Engineering, Data, Business rollout
   - Milestone confidence
   - Dependencies across teams
   - Impact of slippage on downstream work

2. **Budget & Burn**
   - Spend vs plan
   - Burn rate vs delivery progress
   - Cost to complete
   - *Shell: introduced burn-rate tracking; identified over-indexing on spend relative to completion early; corrective action taken*

3. **Capacity & Resourcing**
   - Team capacity across engineering squads, data teams, vendors
   - Critical in shared platform teams serving multiple product areas

4. **Risk & Dependency Impact**
   - Likelihood of risks materialising
   - Impact on timelines and value delivery
   - Cross-team dependency delays
   - Quantifying impact - not just logging risks

5. **Value / Benefits Realisation** *(what many candidates miss)*
   - Expected business outcomes: adoption, usage, efficiency gains, revenue or cost impact
   - *Example: "If rollout slips by 2 months, downstream commercial impact is X"*

6. **Platform-Level Impact**
   - Impact of changes across multiple product areas simultaneously
   - *Example: data model change affecting analytics dashboards, AI models and customer-facing tools*

**Speaking answer - key points to land:**
- Shell: introduced burn-rate tracking against delivery progress → identified over-indexing on spend vs completion → corrective action taken early
- Forecast dependency risks and downstream impact - especially in platform environments where one stream delay affects multiple products
- Link delivery forecasts to expected business outcomes: adoption, operational efficiency

### **Budget Management - Perm vs Contract Resources**

**Q: How do you manage project budgets when the team is a mix of permanent and contract staff?**

**Client-side (Shell, Costa)**
- Perm salaries sit in departmental opex - not in the project budget
- Project budget covers incremental costs only: contractors, vendors, licences, tooling
- Perm time tracked as capacity allocation to flag resource risk - not as a cash line
- Budget management focuses on external spend and burn rate

**Vendor or SI-side (Argos, Vodafone, GSK)**
- All resource time is billable - perm and contract alike
- Staff log hours to timesheets; full team headcount covered by project budget
- Perm staff are a revenue line, not a sunk cost
- Tracked against utilisation, burn rate and margin

**How to frame it in an interview**
- Shell and Costa: managed client-side project budget covering vendor and contractor spend; perm resource tracked as capacity allocation
- Worldpay and Lodestone: managed delivery budgets including internal resource utilisation against client billing
- Both are valid budget management experience - the model differs by which side of the commercial relationship you are on
