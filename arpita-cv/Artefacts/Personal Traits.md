# Personal Traits — Strength, Weakness & Frustration

---

## Global — Delivery Manager

**Role shape:** Squad-level DM, embedded with Product Owner and Tech Lead, fast-moving media and entertainment environment, strong AI/tech curiosity expected.

---

**Strength - making ambiguity navigable at squad level**
- Turn unclear or shifting priorities into a clear plan the squad can execute against
- Costa: redesigned intake process mid-delivery - no PO, worked directly with business functions to bring order to a chaotic backlog
- Shell POS: decoupled two delivery paths under pressure - turned a blocker into a structured choice with a clear way forward
- Comfortable operating before best practice is established - bring structure without slowing the squad down
- *"Where I add most value is making the situation clear enough that the squad can move - even when the situation is still changing"*

---

**Weakness (option 1) - not vocal enough about the team's achievements**
- Always believed the work should speak for itself - instinct is to move to the next problem once something ships
- That works for your own reputation but it is not enough for the team - their contribution needs someone to make it visible
- Working on it: now builds explicit moments to call out individual and team wins; shares milestones with wider stakeholders, not just the programme lead
- *"I've always believed the work should speak for itself - but I've learned that's not enough for the team. They need someone to make their contribution visible, and that has to be deliberate, not assumed"*

**Weakness (option 2) - difficulty saying no in the moment**
- Collaborative by instinct - when a stakeholder raises a good idea it is hard not to absorb it immediately
- In a fast-moving environment, scope can expand quickly if every good idea gets pulled in
- Working on it: now routes new asks through a quick value vs. effort frame before agreeing; separates "yes, now" from "yes, next sprint" from "not this quarter"
- *"I have had to get more deliberate about protecting the squad's focus - enthusiasm for new ideas is a strength until it isn't"*

---

**What frustrates you - late risk surfacing**
- Known risks or blockers that don't travel until the window to act has closed
- Especially in AI and emerging tech delivery where assumptions can become stale quickly - a dependency flagged late in an experimental workflow can invalidate work already done
- It is a process frustration, not a people one - the information was usually there; the cadence to surface it wasn't
- Response: build lightweight risk check-ins into squad rituals so flagging early is the default, not the exception
- *"Late escalation is almost always avoidable - my instinct is to build the habit that prevents it"*
