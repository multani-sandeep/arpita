## Role Context - The Initiative

- **Client:** Royal Mail Group (FTSE100)
- **Initiative:** Improving delivery photo compliance using machine learning - reducing compensation costs paid out for lost parcels
- **The problem:** When a courier delivers a parcel they are required to take a photo as proof of delivery. If the photo does not meet compliance standards (wrong angle, too dark, missing) Royal Mail cannot use it as evidence when a customer claims a parcel was lost - resulting in avoidable compensation payouts
- **The solution:** An ML model assesses whether a delivery photo meets compliance standards. PoC and technical foundation are already in place
- **The role:** Not building the technology - rolling it out, changing frontline behaviour (couriers, delivery offices), tracking compliance metrics and demonstrating commercial impact. The PM is the bridge between the model and the operational reality - getting frontline delivery staff to take better photos, consistently, under time pressure at scale
- **No technical build required** - partner with remote technical/engineering team to deliver outcomes
- **Success metrics:** Reduced compensation costs, improved compliance behaviour, strong stakeholder alignment

**Operational challenges this Royal Mail initiative could face - and how to pre-empt them**

| Challenge | Pre-emptive action |
|---|---|
| **Comms not landing** - broadcast email from ops director ignored by couriers, just like the Costa barista situation | - Design depot-level targeted comms, not company-wide emails - Pilot awareness check before rollout: ask couriers what they know about the change - Build in a briefing step at depot level, not just a digital send |
| **Time pressure** - couriers have hundreds of deliveries per day; stopping to take a compliant photo adds friction | - Work with operations to map the exact delivery workflow step by step - Ensure the photo step takes seconds not minutes - Feed back to the ML team if the compliance bar is creating process slowdown |
| **Poor photo conditions** - bad lighting, awkward angles, weather, large or heavy parcels | - Produce a visual guide showing good vs bad photos in real conditions - Include in training materials, not just a policy document - Share real rejection examples with the ML team to refine the model |
| **Device and connectivity issues** - older scanners or poor cameras; rural areas with poor signal | - Audit device capability and connectivity by depot before rollout - Flag infrastructure gaps to the technical team as a pre-go-live risk - Ensure offline handling is covered in the technical design |
| **Resistance and perception of surveillance** - couriers may see photo compliance as monitoring their behaviour | - Involve frontline staff early - explain the commercial reason (compensation costs protect the business and their jobs) - Frame it as protecting their integrity when a claim is made, not checking up on them |
| **Inconsistent adoption by depot** - some depots compliant, others not - geographical and management variance | - Track compliance rate by depot from day one as the primary leading metric - Identify underperforming depots early and deploy targeted support - Share compliance league tables with depot managers to drive peer accountability |
| **Model rejecting valid photos** - false rejections frustrate couriers and erode trust in the system | - Agree false positive and false negative thresholds with the ML team before go-live - Monitor rejection rates by reason post launch - Create a fast feedback channel for couriers to flag incorrect rejections |
| **No actionable feedback to the courier** - system rejects the photo but courier does not know why or what to do differently | - Include specific rejection reason and corrective guidance as a hard go-live requirement - Test the feedback message with couriers in the pilot before rollout |
| **Workarounds and gaming** - couriers taking photos that technically pass the model but do not genuinely evidence delivery | - Work with operations and the ML team to define what constitutes a valid photo - Include integrity indicators in the model brief - Monitor patterns in accepted photos that correlate with subsequent claims |
| **Change fatigue** - Royal Mail is running multiple tech changes; couriers fatigued by new requirements | - Keep communications brief, visual and relevant to the individual courier - Avoid jargon and technical language in frontline comms - One clear ask: take the photo this way |
| **Data quality at upload** - photos uploaded with wrong parcel ID, connectivity dropouts, corrupt files | - Build data quality checks into the upload pipeline - Monitor upload success rates by depot from day one - Alert operations when a depot's upload failure rate spikes |
