## What Worked
- Honest framing of POS-adjacent experience rather than overclaiming
- 30/60/90 approach directly addressed the ramp-up concern
- Travel flexibility clearly established (children grown up)
- Volunteering answer showed range and AI engagement
- Closing question opened the door for the interviewer to surface doubts

## Improvements / What to Do Next Time
- **Elaborate more in a 1-hour interview**: A 1-hour format gives space to go deeper — use it. Don't give compressed answers when there is time to add texture and credibility.
  - *Example here*: When travel came up, could have named locations already visited (markets, client sites) to reinforce the travel point concretely
  - *Example here*: When seniority or stakeholder engagement came up, could have name-dropped or title-dropped Worldpay colleagues to make the interviewer more comfortable about the level of engagement — e.g., "when I was working with [Name, title] on the TSA exit…"
- **Name/title dropping as a credibility tool**: Especially when returning to a known organisation — the interviewer may know those people, which immediately validates the claim
- Ramp-up answer could have been sharpened with a specific example of how she closed a domain gap quickly in the past (e.g., moving from FMCG data to Loyalty to eCommerce at Shell)
