## Signal Analysis

**Critical signal - Colin's hiring pattern**

Colin has been interviewing for this role for a while and has been rejecting candidates who:
- Ask too many questions about process
- Ask too many questions about structure
- Come across as wanting to impose frameworks or governance overhead

This is the single most important piece of intel from this call. Do not ask about process, governance, ways of working frameworks, or reporting structures in Round 1.

**What Colin is specifically looking for**

- Comfort with ambiguity - flagged explicitly as the primary selection criterion
- Product delivery experience - working closely and collaboratively with a Product Manager/Owner
- End-to-end delivery accountability
- Someone who can bring structure to current ways of working without changing what exists
- Agile coaching capability - but enabling the team, not restructuring the team
- Okay with uncertainty; does not need a clear brief to get started

**Round 1 is a two-way interview**

Mollie explicitly flagged: Colin views Round 1 as a two-way interview, not a one-way assessment. He expects the candidate to ask questions about the role. Arriving with no questions or shallow questions will register as a lack of curiosity or genuine interest.

**Other signals**

- The previous hire (different squad) came from a Costa background - Arpita has Costa experience, which is directly relevant
- Mollie is keen for Arpita to progress; gave explicit preparation guidance (research the website, prepare what excites you about giffgaff)
- Colin is 100% remote (Bristol-based) - comfortable in distributed working environments

## What to Carry Forward into Round 1 Prep

1. **Ambiguity is the primary lens** - every answer should demonstrate comfort operating without a clear brief, inherited process or established structure. This was the gap identified at La Fosse Round 1. It must be nailed here.

2. **Do not ask about process or structure** - ask about product, the squad, the business challenge, what success looks like. Never ask how decisions get made, what the governance model is, or how escalations work.

3. **Prepare questions** - Colin expects them. Minimum 4-5 substantive, curious questions about the product area, the squad, the business transformation and what he is trying to build.

4. **Prepare what excites you about giffgaff** - Mollie explicitly flagged this. Research the website. The answer needs to be authentic and specific, not generic.

5. **Lead with product delivery** - position every story as a collaboration with the PO and engineering team, not a governance exercise.

6. **Employment gap answer** - crisp. One sentence. Forward-looking. Then move on.
