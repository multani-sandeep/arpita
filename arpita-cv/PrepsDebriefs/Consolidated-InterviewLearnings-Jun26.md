# Consolidated Interview Learnings
**Last updated:** June 2026
**Based on:** Elsevier, Waterstones, Worldpay (Round 2), Global, giffgaff, La Fosse / Royal Mail

---

## Before Every Interview

- Identify the primary selection criterion from pre-interview intel (TA call, JD, recruiter notes)
- Write one sentence that lands that criterion inside Tell Me About Yourself - it goes in regardless of format
- Research: company model, culture signals (anti-SAFe, governance-heavy, startup), who is interviewing and their background, recent news
- In lightweight agile or startup cultures, asking about process, governance or reporting structures signals the wrong orientation - ask instead about the product, the squad, the business challenge and what success looks like; in enterprise or regulated environments these questions are appropriate and expected
- Practise the opening and key STAR stories out loud - pacing is a structural risk; slow down, pause after the punchline

---

## Tell Me About Yourself - Master Structure

1. One sentence on the type of delivery you do and the environments you thrive in
2. One concrete ambiguity moment - walked into X with no clear structure, here is what I did (do not wait to be asked)
3. One anchor on cross-functional breadth - product, engineering and business stakeholders
4. One sentence on the gap - crisp, forward-looking, no defensiveness

Do not open with a list of programme types or methodology frameworks. The ambiguity moment must come through unprompted in informal interviews - it will not be asked for directly.

## Recurring Gaps and How to Handle Them

**Empowered product teams vs feature teams (Marty Cagan - Transformed)**
- Read before any role that mentions product operating model, squad-embedded delivery or anti-project framing
- Honest position: understand the model, have operated in hybrid environments, can articulate the distinction
- Do not claim to have run fully empowered product teams if you have not; frame as understanding the direction and having moved teams toward outcome thinking

**Architecture and engineering depth**
- Honest pivot: the PM/DM role centres on delivery and adoption, not platform architecture; cloud platform specifics sit with the engineering team
- Name what you do engage with: data flow design, API dependency mapping, NFRs as delivery constraints
- GCP, specific cloud platforms: "no direct hands-on, but I have worked alongside teams building on Google infrastructure" - do not overstate

**Client-facing commercial delivery (SCDM / account management scope)**
- If probed on commercial scope, anchor to Estee Lauder (checkout conversion and brand commercial outcomes) and Shell (£100M+ transaction volume)
- Seniority language: name who you escalated to and what options you presented; do not just say you escalated

**Senior level gap questions**
- Do not name core delivery competencies (delivery, escalation) as unqualified development gaps at senior level
- Scope development areas specifically to a company's process or governance, not as general capability
- Stronger form: "at Worldpay the escalation matrix was specific to their governance model - that would be the learning curve, not escalation as a skill"

## Format Rules

**Informal / conversational interview (no STAR prompts)**
- Plant the primary criterion in Tell Me About Yourself - do not wait for a question that fits
- The Why This Company answer must close on what you connect with about the company's challenge or ambition - not on what the company offers you
- The concrete doing story must surface unprompted; absence of evidence is read as absence of capability

**Formal / structured (STAR prompts)**
- Answer the question asked; bring evidence when prompted
- Escalation stories: name who, with what (options and consequences, not just the problem), what decision was made
- Stabilise first in live incident questions before moving to fix - show you understand immediate impact before process

## Things to Avoid

- Leading with SAFe in companies with an explicit lightweight agile or anti-framework culture - read the culture signal first; in those contexts leave SAFe in the background; in companies exploring or implementing SAFe it is a differentiator and should be led with
- Process and governance language in companies with an explicit lightweight agile or anti-framework culture - in those contexts it signals the wrong orientation; in enterprise and regulated environments it is appropriate and expected
- "I wouldn't have let that happen" - defensive; answer what you would do when it has happened
- Why this company ending on what the company offers you without closing on what you bring
- Naming delivery and escalation as unqualified development gaps at senior level
- Opening Tell Me About Yourself with a list of programme types or methodology frameworks
- Overloading Tell Me About Yourself - two or three deliberate points maximum; let the questions pick up the rest

## Employment Gap

Handled well from Elsevier onward. One sentence, forward-looking, no defensiveness. Do not over-explain.
Form: "The market has been competitive; I have been applying selectively and volunteering in a PM/delivery capacity in the meantime."

## Questions to Ask (Framework)

Ask about:
- The specific product or initiative the squad is working on
- What success looks like for this role in the first six months
- What the biggest delivery challenge is right now
- What the team dynamic is between DM, PO and engineering
- What the interviewer has found most different about this company versus others

In lightweight agile or startup cultures, avoid asking about:
- Process, ways of working frameworks or governance models
- Reporting structures or escalation paths
- How decisions get made formally

These are appropriate and expected questions in enterprise or regulated environments.

## Bear in Mind

**Overqualified / age dynamic**
One instance (Global, June 2026) with no feedback - not a confirmed pattern, but worth being aware of. In rooms with younger interviewers or where the role level sits slightly below your experience, the concern is less about knowledge and more about retention - will she stay, will she be happy at this level, is she using this as a bridge. The fix is not hiding what you know; it is showing genuine curiosity about their approach, letting them lead occasionally, and signalling authentic interest in the specific work at that level rather than the company name or progression opportunity. Also note: salary conversations with the TA recruiter go into the notes passed to the hiring manager - a flagged expectation above band can plant a retention concern even if salary is not discussed in the interview itself.
