### 6. What would you do if the velocity of a team went up or down?

**Answer given:** [Not captured - assess and investigate]

**Assessment:** This is the most probing of the three velocity questions. Strong answer covers both directions:
- **Velocity drops:** don't react to one sprint; look for root cause - new joiners, increased complexity, unresolved blockers, technical debt; use retro to surface; distinguish between capacity issue and process issue

- Best anchor: Costa - velocity trebled through DoR/DoD adoption, not through pressure or point inflation

### 2. Describe a complex piece of work

**Assessment:** Shell is the right choice for complexity and scale. Sharper answer for future: when requirements are unclear, make the ambiguity visible first - document what is known vs unknown, then bring the right people together to close the gap rather than making assumptions.

### 3. Cross-stream work - developers cannot see how to progress

**Answer given:** Propose a POC or spike to explore options before committing

**Follow-up - What would you expect to see in the outcome of a spike?**
Developers are comfortable with the solution, it does what the change intends, Product Owner can review and is comfortable with the direction

**Follow-up - Have you had to make a technical decision when developers present options?**
- Align to requirements - does it do what is needed
- No unnecessary tech debt being created
- No over-engineered or cowboy coding
- Tactical vs strategic nature of the solution informs how expensive it needs to be
- How long will it take
- Use all these dimensions to form a final recommendation

**Assessment:** Strong answer across all three levels. The spike outcome framing - developer confidence, intent met, PO comfortable - is exactly right. The technical decision framing shows commercial and delivery maturity without overclaiming technical expertise.

### 4. How do you translate delivery status and manage multiple squads without attending every meeting

- Created a self-serve dashboard with high-level metrics per component after being caught off guard giving status mid-task - shared the link with stakeholders and it was well received

**Assessment:** The self-serve dashboard story is the standout - a real example of identifying a gap and solving it proactively. That kind of initiative is what differentiates a strong DM. Likely landed well.

### 8. What is different about AI projects and how would you handle that space (Caroline)

**Answer given:**

**Assessment:** Standout answer of the interview. Caroline asked it from her AI domain and Jack's visible reaction signals it exceeded expectations. ### 10. Go / No-Go checklist

**Assessment:** The "it becomes clear in UAT" framing is experienced and honest. The reopening defects signal is a good practical indicator that goes beyond a checklist.

---

### 11. Conflict with a business stakeholder - Shell S&B POS delay

**Answer given:** Hands were forced to absorb additional scope due to the delay

**Follow-up - How did you make the other team take on the work?**
- Raised with the programme that this was additional scope and needed appropriate time
- Added the work formally to the plan
- Ensured the team was comfortable with it
- Captured the element as a tech debt item

**Follow-up - What if the team says they will never actually pick up the tech debt item?**
- Ensure capacity is always reserved for production issues, platform tech debt and new items - a standing allocation, not a one-off

**Follow-up - What if that team already has 200 tech debt items?**
- Impact-based prioritisation: will the platform struggle, will performance degrade, will we lose the ability to scale

**Assessment:** The follow-up chain probed resilience and commercial judgement. The impact-based prioritisation answer is the right lens and shows maturity beyond just managing the list.

### 12. Conflict with a technical team

**Answer given:** Mediated between two Tech Leads disagreeing on user story documentation - one wanting heavy documentation, the other wanting developer flexibility. Disagreements were disrupting refinement sessions. Called a separate meeting, let each make their case, they could not resolve it. Joked they are probably still arguing to this day.

**Follow-up - What did you learn?**

- Presented each position back to the other
- Asked each to allow the other flexibility to manage their squads in their own way
- Kept focus on the shared goal
- Let them operate differently without getting in each other's way

**Assessment:** The self-deprecating joke landed - a human moment in a competency answer. The learning framing is strong: the distinction between feeling heard and being agreed with is a mark of EQ. Likely landed well with both interviewers.
