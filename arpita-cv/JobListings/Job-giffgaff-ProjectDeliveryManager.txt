At giffgaff, the Project Delivery Manager plays a pivotal role in turning ambitious, cross-team initiatives into reality. Sitting at the heart of our product organisation, you’ll plan, coordinate, and deliver complex programmes that span multiple product teams, external partners, and business functions. Your mission is to keep everyone aligned on shared outcomes and milestones, while protecting the integrity of our Product Strategy and the autonomy of our teams.

You’ll lead the planning and breakdown of large initiatives, working closely with Product Managers and Engineering Managers to map dependencies, manage risks, and keep work moving smoothly across boundaries. From navigating third-party vendors to collaborating with Legal, Finance, and Marketing, you’ll act as the glue that connects teams and translates organisational needs into clear product and technical requirements. You’ll also coach teams on delivery best practices, help prioritise cross-initiative work alongside discovery, and facilitate forums that ensure cadence, quality, and continuous improvement across the delivery lifecycle.

Clear communication is central to the role. You’ll provide concise, outcome-focused reporting on project health, progress, and risks, manage stakeholder expectations in an agile environment, and act as the first point of escalation when cross-team challenges arise. If you thrive on untangling complexity, aligning diverse groups around a common goal, and enabling teams to deliver meaningful outcomes at pace, this role puts you right where giffgaff’s biggest ideas become real.

The must haves

In order to be considered, you must have the following experience;

Strong experience in a Program/Project Management or Delivery role within a technology company, with a focus on software development.
Deep, practical experience operating in an Agile/Scrum/Kanban environment, preferably across multiple teams.
Strong understanding of the difference between empowered Product Teams (Discovery + Delivery) and traditional Feature Teams. Ability to coach teams away from "project thinking".
Ability to engage confidently with Engineering Managers and Engineering Leads regarding technical dependencies, architecture risks, and deployment pipelines.
Proven ability to influence and align teams without direct authority. This role is a true leader who enables, rather than dictates.
