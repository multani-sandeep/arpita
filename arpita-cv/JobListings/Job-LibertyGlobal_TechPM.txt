About the job
We’re looking for a Technical Project Manager to join us in London

This is the role that decides whether our (AI) platform programmes actually land. We work with external build partners – SIs, vendors, specialist firm and we need someone who can run those engagements with real grip. We are looking to a true delivery lead who sets the pace, holds partners to their commitments, and intervenes when things start to drift. You’ll be the connective tissue between our Development and Engineering team, the build partner doing the work, and our external customers, making sure scope, timelines, dependencies and quality stay where they need to be. This is technical project management at the sharper end: you understand what’s being built, you can read a delivery plan critically, and you don’t take a vendor’s word for it when something doesn’t add up.

What will you be doing?
Run external build partners end-to-end – setting expectations, managing delivery against them, and holding partners accountable when scope, timelines or quality slip
Own delivery planning across the AI platform programme – milestones, dependencies, critical path, risk – and keep it accurate as things move
Translate between engineering and the business – turning technical detail into clear status, decisions and trade-offs that stakeholders can act on
Manage SOWs, change requests and commercial conversations with partners – making sure what’s contracted is what gets delivered, and that scope changes are properly governed
Run the governance that keeps decisions moving – steering committees, delivery reviews, risk registers – lightweight enough to be useful, rigorous enough to hold up
Spot problems early and escalate cleanly – surface the issue, frame the options, recommend a path, and drive resolution
Coordinate across OpCos and group functions – keeping the right people informed, aligned and unblocked across multiple markets

Essential

We tend to look for people with:
Strong technical project management experience delivering complex platform or software programmes – you’ve done this in environments where the technology is genuinely complicated
Proven track record of managing external SI and vendor partners – not just attending their status meetings, but actually steering them and pushing back when needed
Enough technical literacy to challenge constructively – you don’t need to write the code, but you need to read an architecture diagram, understand the trade-offs, and know when a vendor estimate doesn’t pass the smell test
Solid commercial instincts – comfortable with SOWs, change control, vendor performance management and the conversations that come with them
Experience working in matrixed, multi-stakeholder environments – keeping things moving across competing priorities and different reporting lines
Sharp written and verbal communication – status that’s actually useful, escalations that frame the decision, updates that respect people’s time

The Kind Of Operator Who


Owns outcomes, not activity – the plan exists to deliver the thing, not to be admired
Is calm under pressure and direct when it matters – won’t let a partner drift to keep the relationship comfortable
Brings rigour without bureaucracy – lightweight process when that’s enough, tighter grip when the stakes demand it
Has good judgment on when to escalate, when to absorb, and when to push back

Who We Are
