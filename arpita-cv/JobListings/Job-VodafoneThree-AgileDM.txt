What You’ll Do 

The Agile Delivery Manager is responsible for developing and leading a high-performing, multi-discipline squad to launch new products and/or enhance existing products, across a range of segments and channels

You will be a qualified and experienced Agile Delivery Manager as well as having experience in managing projects. You are highly collaborative, organised and a strong communicator with the ability to influence all levels. You will have a track record of successful delivery by planning, coaching and managing individuals and/or teams to achieve delivery objectives.

You will ensure your team are able to move forwards by removing blockers, securing capacity, providing seamless communications and working with the Product Owner and/or Product Manager to clarify the product backlog. Key accountabilities include:

Demonstrable experience in product delivery with budgets in excess of £1m
Organising, leading and planning with cross functional teams 
Planning and execution of various agile ceremonies within the squad and in-line with agreed governance 
Ensuring teams complete their deliveries on time using creative thinking, collaboration and to a high level of quality 
Budget/financial management as per guidelines 


Who You Are   

Relevant Agile delivery experience/qualifications, ideally with SAFe experience/qualifications 
Telecommunications experience is highly desirable; we would also consider people with in similar IT agile delivery industry experience. 
Exposure to and understanding of Enterprise IT through managing developments with medium/high customer impacts, large numbers of customers and/or more complex products and services 
Managing external dependencies, working with other Delivery Managers to track and keep on time, articulating issues, dependences and risks to stakeholders 
Consistently facilitating discussion with stakeholders and able to build rapport with stakeholders at all levels 
Creating a safe and mature environment to enable the team to work together, safeguarding the team against unwanted distractions and protect their autonomy
