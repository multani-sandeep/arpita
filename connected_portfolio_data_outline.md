# Connected Portfolio Data for Better Portfolio Management

## Abstract
Brief summary of how collecting and curating connected portfolio data enhances portfolio management processes and organizational outcomes.

## 1. Introduction
- Definition of Connected Portfolio Data.
- The current state of portfolio management data (silos, disconnected).
- The thesis: Connectivity and curation of data lead to superior decision-making and agility.

## 2. The Concept of Connected Portfolio Data
- **Data Integration**: Unifying data from finance, resources, strategy, and execution.
- **Real-time Availability**: Moving from static reporting to dynamic dashboards.
- **Data Quality & Curation**: ensure accuracy, consistency, and contextual relevance.

## 3. Improving Portfolio Management Processes
- **Streamlined Reporting**: Automation of KPIs and metrics.
- **Enhanced Prioritization**: Data-driven trade-off analysis.
- **Risk Management**: Early detection of improved visibility across dependencies.
- **Resource Allocation**: Optimizing utilization based on connected demand-capacity data.

## 4. Outcomes for the Portfolio
- **Agility**: Faster pivot times in response to market changes.
- **Efficiency**: Reduced administrative overhead.
- **Alignment**: ensuring portfolio activities remain tightly coupled with strategic objectives.

## 5. Organizational Benefits
- **Strategic Visibility**: C-suite view of true organizational health.
- **Cross-functional Collaboration**: Breaking down barriers between departments through shared data truth.
- **Predictability & Confidence**: Higher confidence in delivery forecasts and ROI.

## 6. Challenges and Implementation
- Data governance and ownership.
- Technical integration hurdles.
- Cultural shift towards data-driven culture.

## 7. Conclusion
- Restatement of value proposition.
- Future outlook: AI/ML on top of connected data.
