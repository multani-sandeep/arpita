**Reported by:** Nick Solanki & Harshna Ladwa, parents of Shaylan (Shay) Solanki, a Bushey junior player (14)<br>
**Person(s) the concern relates to:** Prad Kanuga — adult, West Herts CC-appointed umpire for the fixture, and parent of a West Herts junior player<br>
**Nature of concern:** Bushey parents allege that, while acting as umpire, Prad Kanuga made repeated derogatory and taunting remarks towards their 14-year-old son during and immediately after his dismissal — including instructing the non-striking batsman to "attack this bowler, he can't bowl" and taunting the boy as he left the pitch. They further allege Prad pressed the boy's mother on at least five separate occasions, during and after the match, for her personal contact details, citing his own safeguarding officer role to justify doing so.<br>
**Type of concern:** Safeguarding (child protection) — an adult in a position of trust/authority (umpire, self-described safeguarding officer) directing repeated derogatory remarks at a 14-year-old and repeatedly seeking his parent's personal contact details.<br>
**Category:** Threshold (not Low Level Concern) — this is a pattern across multiple incidents (repeated remarks during and after the child's dismissal; contact details sought on at least five separate occasions), not an isolated remark, and involves the alleged use of a safeguarding role to justify pursuing a child's family contact details.

**Details of the concern (chronological):**
On 27/06/2026, during the Bushey CC 3rd XI v West Herts CC 5th XI match at King George V Playing Fields, Prad Kanuga (West Herts-appointed umpire) made repeated derogatory remarks towards Shaylan (Shay) Solanki (14) while he was bowling, including instructing the non-striking batsman to "attack this bowler, he can't bowl," and laughing at him. After Shaylan was dismissed, Prad walked alongside him off the pitch and taunted, "Take off your pads and get ready to bowl, if you can," to which Shaylan responded, "If you keep talking to me, I will report you—you're 90 years old and I'm 14, so don't pick on me; pick on someone your own age." During and after the match, Prad pressed Shaylan's mother, Harshna Ladwa, on at least five separate occasions for her personal contact details, citing his own safeguarding officer role to justify the request.

**Witnesses:** Shaylan Solanki (subject of the concern); his mother, Harshna Ladwa (present throughout, directly approached by Prad); other Bushey CC players and officials present at the match (not yet individually named or contacted for corroboration).

**Source of this account:** Reported information — relayed to the club by Shaylan's parents (Nick Solanki & Harshna Ladwa) in their written report of 30/06/2026. Shaylan's own first-hand account has not yet been taken directly by the club; happy to arrange this if it would assist your investigation.

In my view, this raises concerns about the accuracy and fairness of the accounts being provided by West Herts. I never made such a comment to any junior player. The whole purpose of my comment to the captain was to de-escalate the situation at an early stage so that matters did not get out of hand.

This added to my concern that the behaviour towards Shay was not limited to one isolated incident.

I understand that this is part of the complaint made against him, and Hamza has provided his own full account. I later learned that Sachin wanted to speak to Hamza again even after the match had ended, but Hamza politely declined to engage further. I mention this because it does not appear consistent with the suggestion that any of the juniors felt threatened, if they were still seeking a further conversation with Hamza after the game.
>
> I also wish to respond to Prad Kanuga's umpire report. He states that I called him a "cheat". I deny using the word "cheat". I did, however, say to him that he was being dishonest during the boundary incident. I accept that this was a strong comment, but it was made in the context of a decision which, from my position, appeared impossible for him to have seen clearly.
>
> On that boundary decision, I was positioned behind Prad, and I am taller than him. From my position I could not see the painted boundary line clearly. Prad was ahead of me and, in my view, was in an even less favourable position to make a certain call that the ball had crossed the line.
>
> The only legitimate witness who was in a position to say whether the ball had crossed the boundary was the fielder at long-off, Arshad Malik. He is, in my experience, a very honest man, and he clearly stated that it was not a boundary. Despite this, Prad called it a boundary without providing any explanation as to how he had reached that conclusion or how he had been able to see the ball cross the line from his position.
>
> It was therefore a case of whether to accept the account of an honest fielder who was closest to the incident, or to accept the umpire's decision when, from my position, it appeared clear that he could not have seen whether the ball had crossed the line. That is why I challenged the honesty of the decision. I did not call him a cheat. Again, I would like to question the accuracy and intention of the complaint in including such strong words to substantiate their claims.
>
> It is also not accurate to say that Prad simply rescinded his own decision. My recollection is that the decision was changed following the involvement of the leg umpire at the time, Bilal, and the final outcome was that three runs were awarded rather than four.
>

The bowler has played Division 7 cricket and has bowled many overs over the years without such an issue being raised. The captains became involved and the no-ball call was initially annulled. An over later, a junior standing as square-leg umpire also called the same bowler for a no-ball on the same basis. When I asked the junior to explain why he believed it was a no-ball, he was unable to give an explanation.
>
> I want to be clear that I did not intimidate or aggressively challenge the junior umpire. I asked him why he had made the call. In my view, that is a reasonable question given the seriousness of calling a bowler for an illegal action. I do not blame the junior for being put in that position, but I was concerned that Prad had allowed, influenced, or encouraged a junior umpire to make such a serious call without being able to explain it. For the record, this second no-ball call was also annulled after the captains became involved. The fact that the West Herts captain had to become involved in a number of decisions should provide some indication of how disruptive Prad's conduct had become to the smooth running of the game.
>

Saad appeared uncomfortable, and I eventually had to politely ask Prad not to speak to my bowler while he was bowling. In my view, this was inappropriate, particularly given that Saad is also a junior.

Their captain, Dillon Mahawatte, did not help to de-escalate the matter when I first raised concerns about the West Herts juniors sledging our batters. I fully accept that junior players can be enthusiastic and vocal, but senior players have a responsibility to manage that behaviour, particularly when it risks upsetting other juniors involved in the match.
>
> I would also like to make clear that my intention throughout was to prevent the situation from escalating and to protect the welfare of the junior players on both sides. I do not believe that any of my comments were intended or could reasonably be interpreted as threats. They were made in the context of trying to stop continued sledging and inappropriate behaviour during the game.
