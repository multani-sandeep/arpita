## Allegations Raised by West Herts CC (WHCC)
1. **Threat to Saif Azad (16, WHCC junior)** — alleged Bushey player Hamza Wahid said words to the effect of "I will come after you" plus expletives, while WHCC was batting. Saif's own email account and two parent witness statements were submitted in support.
2. **Threat via captain-to-captain exchange** — WHCC captain Dillon Mahawatte alleges Bushey captain Para Sivatharman said, "Just wait. When your youngsters come out to bat, I'm going to get my adults to have a go at them," referring to WHCC junior players. Said to have been witnessed by Prad Kanuga.
3. **Witness statement (Umang Buch)** — describes Hamza directing a threat ("I'll come and get you after the game") at an unnamed 15-year-old player near the boundary; states the child was distressed and was removed from the ground before the match ended as a precaution.
4. **Umpire's report (Prad Kanuga)** — details four in-match incidents:
   - Boundary call at ball 5.3 disputed by Bushey players/captain, reduced from 4 to 3 runs under sustained pressure.
   - Abuse following a wide call at ball 12.2 (sworn at, called "cheat"/"prick").
   - No-ball called for an alleged illegal bowling action (S. Ahmed) at ball 21.6, met with dissent.
   - Bushey players allegedly surrounded and intimidated the 15-year-old square-leg umpire (a WHCC junior volunteer) after he corroborated the no-ball call.
   - The umpire's report separately states **three distinct safeguarding concerns regarding Para Sivatharman's conduct will be submitted separately** — not yet in this folder; follow up to obtain.

## Bushey CC's Response (Para Sivatharman, 29 June 2026 email)
- **Denies** saying "I'm going to get my adults to have a go at them." Says the exchange arose because WHCC juniors (wicketkeeper Sachin Kanuga, gully fielder Kumaran Thangiah) were sledging Bushey senior batter Waseem Saber; Para asked WHCC captain Dillon Mahawatte to intervene, Dillon laughed it off ("Are you going to complain about a 14-year-old making noise?"), and Prad Kanuga then joined the conversation and — in Para's account — spoke disrespectfully and defended the juniors rather than helping de-escalate. Para says his actual comment was a "light-hearted but advisory" warning that Bushey would respond in kind if the sledging continued, not a threat, and that he had cordial exchanges with Sachin and Kumaran afterwards.
- **Wider context on Prad Kanuga's conduct towards Shay Solanki**: alleges Prad ridiculed Shay's batting technique while he batted; after Shay was dismissed, WHCC bowler Champaka Ramanayake (a former Sri Lankan Test cricketer) gave him a send-off and Prad continued the same behaviour; Shay returned to the changing area visibly upset, saying "He's picking on a 14-year-old"; later, while Shay was bowling, Prad made further comments towards him.
- **On the cap/glasses/phone incident with Hamza Wahid**: says Prad (as main umpire) refused to hold Hamza's phone, forcing Hamza to walk to the boundary to leave his belongings and have a word with Dillon; juniors including Sachin Kanuga began commenting during this, Hamza told them to stay out of an adults' conversation and swore unwittingly while doing so — this is the incident Para says underlies the complaint against Hamza. Notes Sachin reportedly wanted to speak to Hamza again after the match ended, which Para says is inconsistent with juniors feeling threatened by him.
- **Denies** calling the umpire a "cheat," but accepts he questioned the umpire's honesty ("dishonest") over the boundary call, citing his own viewing position (behind and taller than Prad) and fielder Arshad Malik's account that the ball did not cross the line; says the call was later changed via leg umpire Bilal's involvement, reducing it from 4 to 3 runs.
- **On the no-ball dispute (S. Ahmad/Ahmed)**: says Prad's throwing call was contested and annulled by the captains; a junior square-leg umpire made the same call an over later on the same basis but couldn't explain it when asked, and it was also annulled. Denies intimidating the junior umpire — says he only asked him to explain the call.
- **Raises counter-concerns**: alleges Prad (umpire, and parent of WHCC's keeper) repeatedly targeted two Bushey juniors — Shay Solanki (as above) and Saad (junior bowler; alleges Prad repeatedly spoke to him and had "hands on Saad's shoulders" while he bowled, until Para asked Prad to stop).
- States a comprehensive statement from Hamza Wahid was prepared in response to the allegation against him (now obtained — see below).

## Hamza Wahid's Statements
**Short rebuttal (30 June 2026, "Re: Child safeguarding")** — addresses specifically the allegation that he threatened a junior player:
- **Categorically denies** threatening any junior player at any stage.
- Argues this is inconsistent with subsequent events: the junior player (Saif Azad) remained at the ground, later acted as square-leg umpire, interacted with Hamza on the field, and shook his hand at the end of the match — points he says are corroborated by an independent witness, the father of another WHCC junior player (Muhammad Burhan) who was present throughout.

**Comprehensive statement** (referenced in Para's email, forwarded within `HamzaEmail.txt`) — fuller account of the match from Hamza's perspective:
- Says WHCC juniors repeatedly showed a lack of respect towards Bushey players throughout the match, and cites an independent witness — the mother of a Bushey junior player — who was present.
- Describes the cap/glasses/phone dispute with the umpire (Prad) in his own words, and a boundary-side conversation with Dillon in which he said Dillon should "act his age" and called the umpire's decision "pathetic" — directed at Dillon, not at any junior, though he says junior players then began shouting from the boundary.
- **Categorically denies** ever saying "I will see you after the game," and says he shook hands respectfully with WHCC players, including Saif Azad, at the end of the match.
- Notes a further incident where Sachin Kanuga (WHCC wicketkeeper) repeatedly approached him during a post-match captains'/umpires' discussion despite being asked to stay out of it.
- Separately addresses Saif Azad's account directly: disputes that Saif left the ground out of fear, noting Saif went on to umpire at square leg, called the disputed no-ball, and shared a friendly exchange with Hamza in Punjabi later in the match — points he says are hard to reconcile with Saif having felt threatened.
- Raises the same counter-concerns as Para regarding Prad Kanuga's conduct towards Shay Solanki and physical contact with Saad, and separately alleges the WHCC bowler (a former Sri Lankan international) threw the ball aggressively after dismissing a Bushey junior.
- Requests written apologies from Saif Azad, Dillon Mahawatte, the umpire (Prad), and his son.

## Bushey CC's Formal Counter-Report (Nick Solanki & Harshna Ladwa, 30 June 2026 email)
Corroborates and substantially expands the counter-allegation against umpire Prad Kanuga, naming Shaylan (Shay) Solanki (14) as the target:
- Alleges Prad made repeated derogatory remarks at Shaylan while he was bowling, including instructing the non-striking batsman to **"Attack this bowler, he can't bowl"** before laughing at him.
- Alleges that after Shaylan was dismissed, Prad walked alongside him off the pitch and taunted, **"Take off your pads and get ready to bowl, if you can."** Shaylan is reported to have responded, **"If you keep talking to me, I will report you—you're 90 years old and I'm 14, so don't pick on me; pick on someone your own age."**
- Alleges Prad repeatedly (on at least five occasions, during and after the match) pressed Shaylan's mother for her contact details, and justified this by citing his position as a safeguarding officer.
- Frames this as **an adult in an official position (umpire, and self-described safeguarding officer) targeting and pressuring a 14-year-old and his parent** — a distinct safeguarding concern in its own right, separate from the earlier counter-allegation about Saad.
- Requests formal review under the League's Code of Conduct.
- Flags that **Bushey U15B play West Herts on Thursday 2 July 2026**, with Prad Kanuga as West Herts team manager for that fixture, and Shaylan and Saad both playing.
