# Purpose — CSO Role, Bushey Cricket Club

This document is my personal reference for what my role as Club Safeguarding Officer requires, so I can check my own practice against it.

**Name:** Arpita
**Role:** Club Safeguarding Officer (CSO), Bushey Cricket Club
**League:** Herts Junior Cricket League

## Core Responsibilities
- Act as the first point of contact for safeguarding concerns involving children and adults at risk within the club.
- Ensure the club's safeguarding policy is up to date, visible, and follows ECB/NSPCC CPSU guidance.
- Maintain accurate, confidential records of any concerns, disclosures, or incidents.
- Liaise with the Herts Junior Cricket League Welfare Officer and statutory agencies (LADO, police, social services) when required.
- Ensure all coaches, volunteers, and committee members in regulated activity hold valid DBS checks.
- Promote safeguarding awareness through induction, training, and regular reminders to members and parents.
- Support the club committee on safe recruitment practices for coaches and volunteers.
- Report annually to the club committee on safeguarding activity and training compliance.
- Confirm the club meets Herts Junior Cricket League affiliation/safeguarding requirements each season.

## Key Relationships
- Club Committee / Chair
- Herts Junior Cricket League Welfare Officer
- Coaches, team managers, and volunteers
- Parents/guardians of junior players

## Record-Keeping Convention
- Every incident gets its own folder in this repo, named `incident-DDMonthYYYY` (e.g. `incident-26June2026`), containing all notes, correspondence, and records for that incident.

## Not Responsible For
- Investigating criminal matters directly (escalate to police/LADO).
- General club discipline unrelated to safeguarding/welfare concerns.
